Architecture overview

How the platform works

A multi-brand analytics platform that consolidates Shopify revenue and Meta / Google / TikTok ad spend across a portfolio of DTC brands into one dashboard and client-ready reports.

Updated 2026
The short answer

The platform runs on a data warehouse. Every connected platform is synced on a schedule into a database; the dashboard and API read from that stored data, not from live platform calls at request time. Because the history is persisted, month-over-month and year-over-year comparisons are simply queries over stored data — not something recomputed live on each request.

The pipeline

Scheduled ingest → warehouse → dashboards & reports. Nothing is queried live or thrown away.

What it connects

Shopify (revenue, orders, inventory, products), Meta Ads, Google Ads, and TikTok Ads — each through its own integration adapter. Auth differs by platform: some are simple (a single token), others are involved — TikTok needs a Business Center app, review and asset endpoints, and Amazon is similar. That complexity is encapsulated per platform, so adding a new one is an isolated, repeatable piece of work rather than a rebuild.

Why history & comparisons work

Every day's figures are normalized into one consistent shape and stored permanently — revenue, spend, orders, refunds, broken down by brand, country, product, campaign and inventory. Month-over-month / year-over-year, dead-stock trends, and re-generating any past month's report all read from that stored history. Backfill jobs load the past on demand, so a brand connected today can still show last year.
