Polar Analytics vs Helm — where each fits

They look like the same thing and aren’t. Polar is a per-brand ecommerce data platform sold to a brand. Helm is an agency’s owned, white-labelable operating system for running many brands at once. The overlap is real; the buyer, the economics and the job are not.

The economics decide it at portfolio scale

Polar is priced and set up per brand. Point that at 100+ stores and the model breaks: even at a conservative blended $600–1,000/mo per store, that’s $0.7–1.2M/year in SaaS fees plus 100 separate onboardings — for a portfolio most of whose stores don’t individually justify an enterprise data warehouse. Helm is one platform Nova owns, at a fixed cost, across every store — and the same build becomes a product you license to other agencies.

Where each genuinely wins

Polar is better at

  • True multi-touch attribution and incrementality (their pixel is the moat)
  • A client-owned data warehouse with raw SQL access
  • No-code, self-service BI for a data-literate in-house team
  • Sending enriched events back to ad platforms + Klaviyo recovery
  • A shipping, maturing AI layer

Helm is better at

  • Running 100+ stores in one place at agency economics
  • Deep, hands-on ad operations across Meta, Google and TikTok
  • Polished, on-brand client reports the agency sends out
  • Being owned — no per-brand SaaS fee eating the margin
  • Being white-labeled and resold as Nova’s own product
Bottom line

Don’t try to out-build Polar’s pixel, Snowflake and AI stack — that’s a funded-startup roadmap and a losing race. Sharpen Helm’s wedge: portfolio economics, ad operations, client-ready reporting, and resale. The two can coexist — Polar for a flagship brand that demands attribution depth, Helm for the portfolio and the agency’s own product.

One gap worth conceding honestly: for a single flagship brand that needs true multi-touch attribution, Helm can’t match Polar today — that’s a real use case where Polar (or a future pixel integration) wins.
